Reading Response #1

One of the things I should have considered more when making my micro sketch was including more instruction/description. I had the text saying generally what to do. In the reading, user experience is discussed a lot. When I consider the games I’ve played, no matter how simple, they still have other elements that improve the look of the game. Part of that as well, was the font for the instructions being different than the pop ups. Feedback is provided with those pop ups. Either it says how there wasn’t enough bites to eat the ice cream or enough to finish it. Unfortunately, it’s not a perfect system. I think Norman would definitely criticize how the ice cream can’t be truly finished. It gives the “done” message just before the ice cream is done every time. This means that it’s never fully complete and that is a point I would say needs to be clarified.

I had intended for there to be a random constraint on the amount of bites that occurred, but only on some tries. I think that it got lost in the creation process, but it would have created some inequality. As a result of this, there really aren’t any constraints. I had also mentioned that I wanted to add not being able to eat one flavor to add more difficulty. If I had figured that feature out, I believe it would have more so guided behavior rather than frustrate the user. If I had, say, made the “person” eating guide which flavor was eaten every single time that may frustrate, especially if there was even more depth like having some extra process to get to the flavors.

If Norman saw my sketch, I think he would say that the experience leaves something to be desired. Simple doesn’t necessarily mean bad, but in my case I could imagine that people would be wanting more almost immediately. He may also comment on my lack of explanation for what specifically is happening. In my mind I know it is ice cream, but that may not be obvious on first impression to the user. Similarly, the situation (i.e. the person eating) is not really explained either. I don’t think it’s particularly necessary, but would add some depth to the sketch. He mentions how people like being able to grasp and understand, for which I think more explanation would be helpful. Overall, I think he may say that there are somethings that could improve the general experience.

What struck me from the text was the part about designing for the way people are, rather than you wish they would be. It’s a simple statement, but designing for people requires you to consider those people and try to gain understanding of the way they are. Not giving thought to those who you’re designing for can lead to accessibility issues or unsuitability overall. This shows in Norman’s example on the power plant. The design caused the technology the workers used to fail all the time because people weren’t part of the equation when creating the mechanisms.
